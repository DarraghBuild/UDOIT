Structuring Your Team and Planning for the Major Project

Chapter 2 in our textbook discusses the process of setting up a virtual team. As a manager in the workplace, you may face the challenge of hiring employees to work on a virtual team; as students in our class, you will have the opportunity to select a group of classmates to collaborate with on the major project for the course. The major project is to write a Research Report analyzing tools for virtual collaboration. If you have not already done so, you should read over the description of the Research Report assignment before completing this module.

The main focus of our class is best practices for virtual teamwork. The major project for our course is for your team to write a Research Report on collaborative technologies (including blogs, wikis, podcasts, and chat tools). Each team member will be responsible for selecting a different technology and proposing it to the team for inclusion in the Report. Then the team will use each of the technologies selected to test its effectiveness for collaborative work. Finally, each team member will be responsible for writing an analysis of one of the technologies.

When setting up your team, you will need to select a team leader or leaders and define the roles of all team members. When roles and responsibilities are clearly defined from the outset of a project, it is much less likely that disagreements will arise or that work will be duplicated or left undone. Do not underestimate the importance of taking the time to come to an agreement regarding the structure of your team and the roles of all team members. The first major decision will be selecting a team leader.

Selecting a Team Leader

In the business world, you typically will not have the freedom to choose your team leader; however, for the purposes of our class project each team will choose its own leader. You have several options for handling the leadership position in your team:

  1. You may choose one student to serve as team leader throughout the entire project.
  2. You may choose two students to share the role of team leader; if you choose this option, you must clearly separate the roles/responsibilities of the two leaders and explain the structure in your Project Proposal.
  3. You may choose to rotate the leadership among all team members; if you choose this option, you must clearly define the time period during which each team member will serve as team leader in your Project Proposal.

If your team fails to select a leader or if a disagreement regarding leadership arises during the course of the project that your team cannot resolve, then Dr. Flammia will appoint a team leader.

When selecting a team leader, consider and discuss with your team members what you expect the leader to do. Then discuss the responsibilities that should be shared among team members. Each team may have its own ideas about what makes a good leader and what a leader should be responsible for within the team structure.

For a team to succeed, it is important that ideas are shared openly at the beginning of the project and that all team guidelines are written up in your team's Project Proposal. The Project Proposal assignment is due on Wednesday, 5/21. The Proposal serves as a kind of contract among team members. In the Team Structure and Management Plan section of the Proposal, you will delineate all aspects of your team's plans for dividing responsibilities, communicating among team members, and handling the decision-making process.

Teambuilding Exercises

Before selecting a team leader, it is important for all team members to express their expectations of a leader and their ideas about the qualities that they would like to see in a leader. It will be easier to select a leader after you have taken a little time to get to know the other members of your team. Before selecting a leader, you may want to engage in one of the teambuilding activities suggested for virtual teams on pages 53-55 in our textbook. These activities may not be necessary if you already know the members of your team from other classes.

It is up to your team whether or not you want to perform one of the teambuilding exercises. However, even if your team chooses not to do so, you should take time to get to know something about one another. Researchers have found that the most successful virtual teams are those teams that establish social communication early in the lifecycle of a project. In order for teams to succeed, team members must build trust in one another. Coppola, Hiltz, and Rotter (2004) offer virtual team members four suggestions for building trust:

  • Establish early communication.
  • Develop a positive social atmosphere.
  • Reinforce predictable patterns in communication and action.
  • Involve team members in tasks.1

1 N. W. Coppola, S. R. Hiltz, and N. G. Rotter, "Building trust in virtual teams," IEEE Transactions on Professional Communication, vol. 47, no. 2, pp. 95-104, 2004.

You do not have to become close friends with your teammates, but you must find a way to build a congenial working relationship. If you do so, you will find it much easier to meet all of the many challenges inherent in virtual teamwork.

In Chapter 2, our textbook offers several suggestions for building a team culture. Your team may use some of these suggestions or may develop your own strategies for creating a positive working relationship. Just keep in mind that making sure your team functions well requires conscious effort; you must take the time to get to know one another and you must strive to understand and respect viewpoints different from your own. Virtual teamwork is work that requires effort, but putting in the effort at the start of a project can ensure a positive, productive, and enjoyable team experience.

Team Structure and Management Plan

In this section you should describe the division of labor within your team, specifically stating which responsibilities each team member will have. Research Report (25 points) Due by 11:59 pm Thursday, 6/19

Description of Project

The team project is a Research Report on collaborative technologies (including blogs, wikis, podcasts, and chat tools). Each team member will be responsible for selecting a different technology and proposing it to the team for inclusion in the Report. Then the team will use each of the technologies selected to test its effectiveness for collaborative work. Finally, each team member will be responsible for writing an analysis of one of the technologies.

The analysis should cover a discussion/description of the advantages and disadvantages of using each technology for communication and collaborative work. The analysis of each technology should include references to journal articles, white papers, and textbooks, as well as the team's experiences with the technologies.

The Research Report will include an analysis of one technology per team member. The entire team must review and approve each member's individual analysis. In addition to the individual analyses, the Report should include a brief introduction and a conclusion that draws together the main points of the Report and makes recommendations for how these technologies could be used for virtual team collaboration specifically, and for team work in general.

The format of the Research Report should be based on the Research Report Template available under the Materials Button on the Course Home Page.

Evaluation Criteria

The Research Report will be evaluated based on its content, format, and writing style.

Fifteen points will be awarded for the content. The content should include a thorough discussion of each tool evaluated by the team. The coverage of the tools should be consistent; that is to say, each tool should be discussed in approximately the same depth and include a well-developed analysis of the advantages and disadvantages of using each technology for communication and collaborative work. Further the analyses should include references to journal articles, white papers, and textbooks, as well as the team members' own experiences with the technologies.

Ten points will be awarded for the presentation of the information (format and writing style). The content of the Report should be well organized with a clear hierarchy of information. The Report should follow the template for the assignment. The writers should strive to integrate their separate research into a Report that has a unified voice. The writing style should be consistent. The references should be presented using the MLA style for referencing sources. The references section must be accurate and complete.
