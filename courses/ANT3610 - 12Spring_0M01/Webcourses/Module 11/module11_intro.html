Module 11: Endangered Languages

Introduction

Welcome to the next to last module of the semester! This module is extremely important and interesting, but you will also find it a lot less challenging in terms of reading load than the last two. Moreover, this module will be extended over two weeks, with no graded discussion component in the first week. In this module we will learn about language extinction, its reasons, mechanisms, and circumstances, as well as language revitalization movements and the ways in which some languages can and have been brought back from the brink of extinction. You will also learn about why linguistic anthropologists place so much importance on the negative aspects of language extinction, despite the fact that language extinction is an extremely common occurrence.

As you complete this module, do the readings, and watch the film The Linguists, think about the role power asymmetries play in the disappearance of languages and the possibilities of agency in everyday life (more on this in Module 12...)

Objectives

At the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • List examples of disappearing languages.
  • Identify the criteria for classifying languages as safe, extinct, or somewhere in between.
  • Identify the mechanisms by which languages become extinct.
  • Explain the position of linguistic anthropologists regarding the extinction of languages.
  • Identify language revitalization movements.
