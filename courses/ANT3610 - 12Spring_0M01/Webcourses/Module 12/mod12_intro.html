Module 12: Language, Power, and Agency

Introduction

Welcome to the final module of the semester!!!! This module is all about looking forward: you will learn some challenging concepts such as Bourdieu’s practice theory and Foucault’s take on power and discourse. These are concepts that are salient not only in linguistic anthropology, but in anthropology in general. These theories integrate linguistic anthropology into the broader field of general anthropology and give you a sense of where the field is headed right now. In the spirit of exposing you to the more cutting-edge side of linguistic anthropology, I also assigned Leila Monaghan’s article “The Expanding Boundaries of Linguistic Anthropology: 2010 in Perspective.”

I really hope that you have enjoyed taking this class as much as I have enjoyed teaching it. This was the first time I have taught the course in mixed mode, so I apologize for any kinds of glitches that may be happened a result. I always love getting feedback because it gives me the ability to improve my teaching in future classes. I do hope that I see some of you again.

Please feel free to contact me if you ever feel so inclined. It has been a real pleasure getting to know you as my students. Have an amazing break and a wonderful, successful career at UCF.

Objectives

At the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • List the major theories of power and their corresponding theorists.
  • Summarize the variety of ways in which Linguistic anthropology is changing and expanding (cf. Leila Monaghan’s article).
  • Explain Ahearn’s approach to power (how she brings these theories together).
