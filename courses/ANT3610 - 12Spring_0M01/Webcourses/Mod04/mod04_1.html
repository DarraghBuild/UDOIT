Module 4: Language, Thought, and Culture

Introduction

What is the relationship between language and thought? How does culture influence –and how is culture influenced by—language? Think about how your own upbringing has endowed you with a specific language(s) and worldview. Now, think about the example of the two Maya men greeting each other in the course introduction. What can language tell us about worldview? Can you have a worldview without a corresponding language for it? In this chapter, we will learn the history of this debate in the field of anthropological linguistics, and consider some compelling research that has been carried out on these questions.

In this module, you will find a guide to the readings assigned as well as a supplementary video that clearly illustrates a False-Belief test. Please be sure to careful read the reflection question I pose at the end of this module, and allow them to inform how you frame your original discussion post.

Module Objectives

At the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • List the theories of language and thought most commonly used in linguistics and linguistic anthropology
  • Present specific examples of Whorfian effects in different languages.
  • Explain the central debates around the topic of language and thought, particularly the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis.
  • Analyze recent research on the influence of language on thought.
