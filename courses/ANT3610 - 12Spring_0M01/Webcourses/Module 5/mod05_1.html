Module 5: Communities

Introduction

What exactly is a “community” and why is this term important to linguistic anthropologists? This module deals explicitly with the ways in which linguistic anthropologists frame and conceptualize groups of people who use language. It uncovers some of the assumptions and unstated social theories that underpin commonly used concepts such as speech community or community of practice, and will introduce you to some of the most interesting research that furthers our understanding of the relationship between linguistic practices and social groups.

Objectives

After completing this module, you should be able to:

  • Define speech community and community of practice. Explain how these concepts differ from one another.
  • List Gumperz’ criteria for identifying a speech community.
  • Describe some of the criticisms made of the speech community model.
  • Name alternatives proposed to the concept of speech community.
  • Explain the criteria for defining a community of practice.
  • Understand an example of close conversation analysis.
  • Analyze the merits both the speech community and communities of practice models.
  • Evaluate the appropriateness of each concept for pursuing specific kinds of research questions
