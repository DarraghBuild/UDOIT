Assignment Overview

This week we have a MAJOR discussion. This discussion exercise is worth 100 points of your final grade and is considered an important component of your required work so far this semester. The primary goal of this assignment is to measure your ability to summarize, analyze, and apply the major concepts, theories, and ethnographic examples we have covered thus far this semester to a stand-alone ethnography. You may select any of the themes or topics we have covered thus far, or even a combination. Your primary objective is to take a topic (multilingualism, race, communities of speakers, etc) and use the theories associated with the topic (Butler's take of performativity, Bakhtin's heteroglossia, etc) to analyze Basso's argument and data.

Original Post (60 points)

In your groups, you will have a thoughtful conversation about Basso's Portraits of the Whiteman. Your initial post should indicate the topic you have chosen in its title (e.g., "Basso and Performativity"). Rather than simply posting a reaction to the reading, you must consider how the particular theoretical framework you've chosen can be used to re-analyze Basso's data or to critically engage with his argument. In order to do this, you must also demonstrate that you understand what Basso's argument and/or data are.

  • Your post should include something along the lines of:

    "In Portraits of the 'Whiteman', Basso argues/states/presents evidence that..."

  • followed eventually by:

    "In this post, I suggest/argue/show that Butler's performativity [for example] is useful in/critical of/contradicts considering Apache joke-telling performances because..." You must cite appropriately and demonstrate a clear grasp of the themes and the appropriateness of the literature cited to your argument.

You must post your original discussion post no later than Midnight on Wednesday.

Reply Post (40 points)

You must post a reply to a colleague in your group. Your reply must demonstrate that you understand your colleague's argument and either build on it using relevant literature or respectfully disagree with it citing appropriate literature. The idea is to demonstrate that you can have an intellectual exchange based on the texts, theories, and concepts we have read and learned about this semester.

  • For example, your reply could say something along the lines of:

    "Your use of Butler's ideas of performativity is really interesting. This comparison might be even more productive if you introduce the Ahearn's discussion of diglossia, which introduces the notion of hierarchy and power into the practice of language."
